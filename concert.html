7th Grade Program Notes and Recordings

Fanfare, Ode and Festival by Bob Margolis

Program notes from JW Pepper: Adapted from 16th century Renaissance wind music and carefully scored for the modern concert band, these three masterfully contrasted and very playable dances provide you with stunning results, fresh sounds, pleasant harmonic surprises and material that is rewarding to rehearse, to perform, and to hear!

A Quiet Rain by Walter Cummings

Program Notes from JW Pepper: As the brass perform a melancholy tune, the subdued sounds of delicate woodwind and percussion parts create the gentle rain effects in this wonderful tone poem for younger bands. This is really quite playable by young musicians, yet the sophistication of the musical ideas will help your students experience the power of music to evoke images.

Ancient Voices by Michael Sweeney

Program Notes from the Canaseraga Band Program: This composition is suggestive of the moods and sounds of early civilizations. The music has not been written in an attempt to depict any specific culture or continent. The sounds you hear however, may bring to mind early populations that may have used the bones of Mammoths and other animals as musical instruments. Michael Sweeney has incorporated a number of unique performance techniques during this piece such as using a classroom recorder, tone clusters, pencil tapping, and even singing.

8th Grade Program Notes and Recordings

Kentucky 1800, Clare Grundman

Kentucky 1800 is based on the tunes of three American folk songs: The Promised Land, Cindy, and I'm Sad and I'm Lonely, melodies which are reminiscent of the years the pioneers were forging westward. It is a rhapsodic tone poem in which thematic material is treated in great depth and with variety. The work is a band masterpiece which has long since become a favorite with audiences.

A Hymn For Band, Hugh Stuart

Hugh M. Stuart was born in Harrisburg, Pennsylvania. He received his music training from Oberlin Conservatory of Music, Columbia Teachers College, Rutgers University, Newark State College, and the University of Michigan. He taught instrumental music in the schools of Maryland and New Jersey for 33 years. Stuart wrote more than 100 published compositions, arrangements, method books, band and orchestral collections, solos, and ensembles in the educational field. A Hymn for Band has steadily become one of the classic repertoire pieces for developing bands. This beautiful work is rich in melodic content, varied scoring, and marvelous counterlines and harmonies.

Train Heading West, Timothy Brodege

Program Notes from Teaching Music through Performance in Beginning Band:Train Heading West and Other Outdoor Scenes is a three-movement work portraying various outdoor scenes in late nineteenth and early twentieth century America. It was originally composed as a set of three sketches for beginning band in the mid~1970s, and was revised and expanded in 1997.Timothy Broege was born in Belmar, New Jersey, on November 6, 1947. Following early studies in piano and music theory, he earned a Bachelor of Music degree at Northwestern University in 1969, where he studied composition.

March of the Irish Guard, James Ployhar

Program Notes from JW Pepper: A spirited march treatment of the tune known as The Boys Of Wexford, this lively arrangement really moves along! After a contrasting middle section in 6/8 featuring bagpipe effects, the opening returns to finish off the piece with a grand, fully scored climax.
